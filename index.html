Lung Disease Detection with SHAP

Undergraduate thesis · UIN SUSKA Riau

role: Sole researcher period: 2025 data: 3,030 chest X-ray images
the problem

Deep-learning models for medical image classification often function as black boxes — accurate, but clinically risky without insight into why a prediction was made. The goal: a chest X-ray classifier accurate enough to be useful, with predictions a clinician could actually audit.


This project explores the application of Explainable AI (XAI) using SHAP to improve the interpretability of a deep learning model for chest X-ray disease classification. A VGG-16-based model was trained to classify chest X-ray images into COVID-19, Normal, and Pneumonia. SHAP visualizations were then used to highlight the image regions that contributed most to the model's predictions, providing insight into the decision-making process and increasing model transparency.

pipeline

    Dataset

    Curated a 3,030-image chest X-ray dataset spanning three classes: COVID-19, Pneumonia, and Normal.

  2. 02

    Model training

    Trained a VGG-16 convolutional network via transfer learning to classify chest X-rays across the three classes.

  3. 03

    Explainability

    Applied SHAP to visualize which regions of each X-ray drove the model's prediction — inspectable, not just trusted.
